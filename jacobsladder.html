Jacob's Ladder

2024

A classic high-voltage project that creates a rising arc of electricity between two diverging electrodes. The arc starts at the bottom where the electrodes are closest together, then climbs upward as the heated air rises, until it breaks at the top and restarts at the bottom.

Built using a high-voltage transformer, this project was a great way to learn about electrical safety, high-voltage systems, and the physics of electrical arcs. The distinctive buzzing sound and visual effect make it one of the more dramatic electronics projects I've built.

How it works

  • High-voltage transformer generates thousands of volts
  • Arc forms at the narrowest gap between electrodes
  • Heated air rises, carrying the arc upward
  • Arc breaks at the top, cycle repeats
